If you are thinking of applying to Cambridge and would like to know more about CUAC, please fill in the form below. As the 3rd largest sports club in Cambridge, we have athletes from a wide range of colleges and subjects, so we can easily put you in touch with a subject buddy (namely, someone studying the same subject as the one you want to study at Cambridge) and/or a college buddy (someone at the college you want to apply to). Things I wished I knew before I came to Cambridge - from an athlete's perspective

What's the best college to apply to if I want to do athletics?

Any of them! Pretty much all colleges have athletes from CUAC, and since the way we train and compete in athletics is mainly on a university-wide level (as opposed to rowing, for example, where most athletes will train with their college team and coaches and compete against other colleges), which college you go to really doesn't matter. Maybe it might be worth considering a more central college to be quite close to the track at Wilberforce Road (first year accommodation at Clare for example is only a five minute cycle away) - but then again, in recent years we've had a high turnout of athletes from the colleges further out. Who knows, maybe the slightly longer cycle ride builds better athletes? Irregardless of which college you choose, you will be massively involved in the club and get to know athletes really easily from other colleges because of the fact that we do operate on a university-wide level, so choose whichever college you like!

But aren't there some colleges that are more likely to accept me, and are there some colleges that look more highly upon sports than others in the application process?

From experience, we would say that its not worth second guessing which college to apply for based on which one is more likely to accept you (both in terms of admissions statistics and in terms of extracurricular preferences). Just be yourself, and yes, let the fact that you are interested in sport shine through in your application, but don't worry too much about which college has a 'sportier' reputation or is better for your subject. Do get in touch, however, if you'd like to find out more about what a particular college is like as we can easily and very quickly put you in contact with an athlete that currently goes to that college so that you can get an insider's perspective on studying there.

I currently do athletics at X. How would it work me being a part of both clubs?

Great question. You could keep your home club as your first-claim club, and CUAC as your second-claim and compete and train with both. You wouldn't have to pay the England Athletics fee again since you would have already paid it with your home club. You would have to pay our membership fee though, but that gets you access to high-quality coaching, our amazing facilities at the track at Wilberforce road as well as strength & conditioning facilities at the University Sports Centre, and also travel to major competitions throughout the year.

Can you give me advice on my application?

Whilst we cannot offer formal advice on the application process, the university website has lots of useful information about applying to Cambridge. If you have any questions or would like further insight into the information they make publicly available from a current student's perspective, feel free to get in touch with us. We would be more than happy to share with you our own experiences, but of course please note that we can only offer advice relative to what we have seen and heard and are not able to offer formal interview training/preparation.

What if I want to do multiple sports during my time at Cambridge?

As athletics is such an excellent sport for developing the foundational strength, speed and skill needed for almost any other sport, CUAC have several members who participate in multiple sports across the university. If playing sport at Cambridge sounds like something you'd be interested in, and you'd like to find out more about the university wide facilities and sports available, have a look at the Sport @ Cambridge website.
